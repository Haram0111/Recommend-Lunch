한국 음식 소개

삼겹살(Samgyeopsal)

It is a Korean grilled dish made by grilling pork belly. It is named because the flesh and fat parts are overlapped three times. They grill it without seasoning, dip it in salt or ssamjang, and wrap it in vegetables. In Korea, there is a theory that pork belly is helpful when you inhale a lot of dust into your lungs. Therefore, miners have eaten a lot of pork belly since before, and in recent years, the consumption of pork belly has soared whenever yellow dust blows. However, there is no sufficient medical basis for the theory that pork belly or pork oil prevents or treats a lung-related disease.

불고기(Bulgogi)

Marinate the beef in a sauce made of soy sauce, honey, chopped green onions, garlic, sesame salt, and pepper, and then grill it. They also mix rice with their own soup. From the Joseon Dynasty to the 1940s, Bulgogi was similar to Neobiani without soup, and it is estimated that the soup would have changed to its own form by the end of Japanese colonial era. Since the 1960s, the perception that a dish named Bulgogi refers to a meat dish with soup has changed, but Gwangyang Bulgogi or Eonyang Bulgogi still maintains the same shape as Neobiani Grilled.

김치찌개(Kimchijjigae)

It is a soup dish using kimchi. It is a food that is boiled spicy with pork, tofu, chopped green onions, and onions in kimchi. Instead of pork, they sometimes boil it with cans of tuna. The history of kimchijjigae actually begins around the same time as kimchi. There is a theory that it is difficult to just eat it because it is too sour and salty, or that the way kimchi was boiled in water to soak up the amount has become the current kimchi stew. The most important thing is to use kimchi that is ripe enough to taste sour.

된장찌개(Doenjangjjigae)

It is a soup dish made by doenjang (a paste made by aging fermented soybean lumps made by boiling beans) as the main ingredient. It is a food boiled with soybean paste and meat, shellfish, tofu, zucchini, potatoes, and enoki mushrooms. There are cases where beef is added to meat, and there are cases where pork is added. The main ingredient is tofu, and the rest can be added according to your preference. The soup is thick and various ingredients are combined, so it tastes good even if you mix rice. It is also boiled spicy with red pepper paste.

비빔밥(Bibimbap)

It is a traditional Korean food mixed with various vegetables in rice and red pepper paste or soy sauce. Usually, bean sprouts, carrots, zucchini, and mushrooms are on top. Also, meat, eggs, and sesame oil may be added depending on your preference. In Joseon, which was a Confucian country, there is an aspect of applying the five colors caused by the five elements of yin and yang even when eating food. As a result, the basic table setting itself had to be diversified, and due to the nature of the agricultural society, it was difficult to bring the lunch table to the rice field, so there is a theory that the same form as bibimbap would have come out.

제육볶음(Jeyukbokkeum)

It is a food that is marinated and stir-fried in red pepper paste sauce with ginger juice, thinly sliced pork. Generally, use pork front legs or hind legs and add vegetables such as onions, carrots, perilla leaves, and cabbage to your liking. Usually, Jeyukbokkeum is served with rice, or it is often served in the form of a bowl of rice with Jeyukbokkeum on top. The original meaning of 'Jeoyuk' means pork. It is a relatively short-lived food that became popular only in the mid-1980s, starting with a restaurant without a signboard in Jung-gu, Seoul. Usually, it is often seen in places with a large floating population, such as near universities, near companies, and driver's restaurants.

김밥(Gimbap)

It is a food that white rice with salt and sesame oil, spread it thinly on a lightly baked seaweed, and roll it around with spinach, carrots, and pickled radish, and cut it into appropriate sizes. The ingredients that go inside can be put in various ways to suit your taste. Until the 1980s, vinegar was added to the rice in gimbap. This is because gimbap at that time was a special food to pack when going on a picnic rather than a daily meal. Gimbap wrapped in a lunch box outdoors was easy to spoil, so vinegar, which served as a preservative, was added. Throughout the 1980s, sesame seeds, salt, and sesame oil were added instead of vinegar and gradually replaced with mixed rice.

떡볶이(Tteokbokki)

It is one of the representative street foods as it is stir-fried with red pepper paste seasoning with vegetables and fish cakes in a bite-sized rice cake. Depending on your preference, you can add ramen noodles, udon noodles, cheese, and sausages. The most common method is red tteokbokki using red pepper paste, and there is also a method using seasonings such as soy sauce, ketchup, curry, jjajang, and cream sauce. In Joseon, it was eaten as a royal cuisine with soy sauce mixed with meat, vegetables, and stir-fried rice cakes, but these days it is called "Gungjung Tteokbokki."

삼계탕(Samgyetang)

It is a food made by putting ginseng, jujube, chestnuts, glutinous rice, and garlic in the belly of a young chicken. It is a traditional summer health food in Korea. Although the history of eating ginseng with ginseng is short, "Dakbaeksuk," which can be seen as the originator of Samgyetang, has been eaten since the Three Kingdoms period and existed during the Joseon Dynasty. Japanese colonial era It is the beginning of samgyetang today that was made by adding ginseng powder to baeksuk or chicken soup. In the 1960s, with the spread of refrigerators, ginseng could be stored for a long time, and it changed to adding dried ginseng instead of ginseng powder.

찜닭(Jjimdak)

It is one of the Korean chicken dishes, which is boiled with various vegetables shredded in sliced chicken and seasonings based on soy sauce, and then boiled with cellophane noodles mixed and boiled. Andong-si's Andong Jjimdak is famous, and the Jjimdak with cellophane noodles is not that old.There is a theory that the origin of jjimdak is an improvement of traditional food that has originally been handed down. Originally, the spicy one with Cheongyang pepper was the original one, but the sweetness was strongly modified to suit the taste of Seoulites. Therefore, it is quite spicy when eaten in Andong Jjimdak Alley.

부대찌개(Budaejjigae)

It is a soup dish made by boiling ham, sausage, bacon, baked beans, pork, and tofu as the main ingredients in a thick kimchi seasoning soup, and is also eaten with ramen noodles. In general, it is also known as the "Korean Army Stew" because it is boiled with ham, sausages, and baked beans that came through the U.S. military in the midst of food shortages immediately after the Korean War. It is a unique food that clearly maintains the identity of Korean cuisine called spicy stew, while it is a fusion of foods from various countries, and is also considered a symbol of modern Korean history itself during the turbulent era.

잡채(Japchae)

It is a dish in which boiled cellophane noodles, carrots, onions, spinach, mushrooms, and meat are stir-fried in oil, and boiled glass noodles are stir-fried together with soy sauce seasoning. The chewy texture of cellophane noodles and the harmony of vegetables and meat are not stimulating, and are usually served on the table during festivals and holidays. Korean japchae originated from Chinese japchae. Japchae was handed down during the Joseon Dynasty, and contrary to China, where japchae was a popular dish, it settled as one of the rare royal cuisine in Joseon. Since it was a dish that served on the Surat table, various specialties were put in.
